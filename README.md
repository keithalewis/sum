# Simple Unified Model

How to value, hedge, and manage the risk of _any_
collection of instruments.

There is a huge, unsolved problem in finance: How to value, hedge, and
manage the risk of portfolios of any collection of instruments.
Historically, banks and hedge funds have desks dedicated to
each product area such as
equities, fixed income, commodities, FX, etc. The modern world
requires a framework for understanding the correlation between these.
A common approach is to specify a copula. The Gaussian copula
led to the infamous 2008 debacle in structured credit markets.

Copulas are the wrong tool for this problem.
Stephan Ross showed the way. This project extends his work by
adding an explicit knob for cash flows.

For full details see https://keithalewis.github.io/math/sum.html

If you know about finance but are not a mathematician see https://keithalewis.github.io/math/ross.html

The talk I gave at the Museum of Mathematics in December 2025 is
available at https://kalx.net/sum.html.

## Model

Let $T$ be a finite totally ordered set of _trading times_,
$I$ a finite set of _market instruments_,
$\Omega$ be the set of possible outcomes, and $(𝒜_t)$ be partitions of $\Omega$ representing information available at time $t\in T$.

### Price

We let $\Omega_X = (𝒁^I)^T$ be the set of
all functions from $T$ to the integers
indexed by instruments.
The point $(t,x)$ represents the prices
at time $t$. The price of instrument $i$, $X_t(i)$,
is $x(i)$ times the minimum trading increment of instrument $i$.

Note we do not assume the price
of an instrument is a real number and use its exact value.

### Cash Flow

We let $\Omega_C = (𝒁^I)^T$ be the set of
all functions from $T$ to the integers
indexed by instruments.
The point $(t,c)$ represents the cash flows
at time $t$. The cash flow of instrument $i$, $C_t(i)$,
is $c(i)$ times the minimum payment increment of instrument $i$.

Note we do not assume the cash flow
of an instrument is a real number and use its exact value.

### Trading

We let $\Omega_\Gamma = (𝒁^I)^T$ be the set of
all functions from $T$ to the integers
indexed by instruments.
The point $(t,\gamma)$ represents the number of shares
traded at at time $t$.
The number of shares of instrument $i$, $\Gamma_t(i)$,
is $\gamma(i)$ times the minimum share increment of instrument $i$.

A trading strategy is an increasing sequence of stopping times $\tau_j$
and number of shares to trade $\Gamma_j\colon 𝒜_{\tau_j} \to 𝒁^I$.

The position at time $t$ is
$\Delta_t = \sum_{\tau_j < t} \Gamma_j = \sum_{s < t} \Gamma_s$
where $\Gamma_s = \Gamma_j$ if $s = \tau_j$ for some $j$ and 0 otherwise.
Note the strict inequality. It takes some time for trades to settle.

Define the value at time $t$ of a trading strategy by
$V_t = (\Delta_t + \Gamma_t)\cdot X_t$
and the amounts generated by the strategy $A_t = \Delta_t\cdot C_t - \Gamma_t\cdot X_t$.

### Accounting

Define the _value_ (or _mark-to-market_) at time $t$ of a trading strategy by
$V_t = (\Delta_t + \Gamma_t)\cdot X_t$. It is how much you get from liquidating
the existing position and the trades just done at current market prices.
Note $\Delta_t + \Gamma_t = \Delta_{t + \epsilon}$ for sufficiently small $\epsilon > 0$.

The _amounts_ generated by the trading strategy are
$A_t = \Delta_t\cdot C_t - \Gamma_t\cdot X_t$. You get cash flows proportional
to your existing position and pay for the trades just executed.

## Arbitrage

Arbitrage exists (in this model) if there is a trading strategy
$(\tau_j, \Gamma_j)$ with $A_{\tau_0} > 0$,
$A_t \ge 0$ for $t > \tau_0$, and $\sum_j \Gamma_j = 0$.
You make money on the first trade, never lose money after that,
and eventually manage to close out. The last condition prevents
Nick Leeson strategies of just borrowing money until you put
a 350 year old bank out of business.

Note this definition of arbitrage does not involve probability.

Every arbitrage-free model has the form 
$$
X_t D_t = M_t - \sum_{s < t} C_s D_s|{𝒜_t},
$$
where $M_t$ is a vector-valued martingale measure
($M_t = M_u|𝒜_t$ for $t\le u$) and $D_t$ is the stochastic discount.
(Any positive finitely-additive measures $D_t\in ba(𝒜_t)$ can be used.)

This implies

$$
X_t D_t = (X_u D_u + \sum_{t < s \le u} C_s D_s)|_{𝒜_t}\quad\text{(1)}
$$

In the case of zero cash flows this says $(X_t D_t)$
is a martingale measure (Black-Scholes/Merton).
If $X_u D_u$ goes to zero as
$u$ goes to infinity, this says price is the discounted
value of future cash flows (Graham-Todd).

A consequence of (1) and our accounting definitions is

$$
V_t D_t = (V_u D_u + \sum_{t < s \le u} A_u D_u)|_{𝒜_t}\quad\text{(2)}
$$

Note how price in (1) corresponds to value in (2)
and cash flows in (1) corresponds amount in (2).

**Trading strategies create synthetic market instruments.**

## Hedging

If a cash-settled derivative pays $\hat{A_j}$ at
increasing stopping times $\hat{\tau}_j$ then its value
at time $t$ is determined by

$$
V_t D_t = (\sum_{\hat{\tau}_j > t} \hat{A_j} D_{\hat{\tau}_j})|_{𝒜_t}.\quad\text{(3)}
$$

_if_ we can find a trading strategy with $A_t - \hat{A}_j 1(t = \hat{\tau}_j) = 0$
for all $t\in T$. [^1]

[^1]: In general this is not possible. An area for future research
is to find trading strategies making the difference
white noise with minimum variance.

Since $V_t = (\Delta_t + \Gamma_t)\cdot X_t$ we have the
Fréchet derivative $D_{X_t} V_t = \Delta_t + \Gamma_t$.
Since the position at time 0 is $\Delta_0 = 0$ this gives us
our first trade $\Gamma_0 = D_{X_0} V_0$.
At times greater than 0 we have $\Gamma_t = D_{X_t} V_t - \Delta_t$.
Since $\Delta_t$ is the accumulation of prior trades, 
this determined $\Gamma_t$. What this _does not_ determine is
when to trade. If we trade at positive integral multiples of some
fixed $\Delta t$ and let $\Delta t \to 0$ then we get the usual
Black-Scholes/Merton continuous time delta hedging strategy.

## Implementation

$A_\tau$ at stopping time $\tau$ is determined
by 
$$
V_t D_t = (A_\tau D_\tau 1(\tau > t))|_{𝒜_t}
$$

A stopping time is a function $\tau\colon\Omega\to T$
with $\{\tau = t\}\in 𝒜_t$ for $t\in T$.

We have
$$
V_t D_t = \sum_{t\in T} \sum_{A\subseteq\{\tau = t\}} A_\tau(A) D_\tau(A) 1(t > \tau)
